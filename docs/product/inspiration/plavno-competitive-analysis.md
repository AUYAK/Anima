## Direct Competitors (Russian Market)

### WhereToEat (wheretoeat.ru)
- Strengths: 12 years legitimacy, respected by industry
- Weaknesses: No mobile app, dated UX, no ML, no gamification
- Position: Oldest independent guide since 2012

### GreatList (greatlist.ru)
- Strengths: Best UX among Russian guides, strong Telegram (200k+)
- Weaknesses: Small team, no ML quiz, no social layer
- Position: Author-driven (Sysoev FM)

### Яндекс Ultima (ultima.guide)
- Strengths: ML infrastructure, 126 experts, 100M+ distribution via Yandex ecosystem
- Weaknesses: Not independent, no transparent methodology
- Position: Part of Yandex Eats — "Strategic Risk #1" for Plavno

### Lemon Guide (lemonguide.ru)
- Strengths: Premium positioning, strong in St. Petersburg
- Weaknesses: Weak technology
- Position: Launching Moscow fall 2026 as direct competitor

## Global Reference Models

### Michelin Guide
- Authority since 1900; 1-3 star system
- Plavno adopted as: Рекомендовано / Отмечено / Лауреат
- Exited Russia 2022 — left a premium authority gap
- Weakness: Outdated UX, no ML, no gamification

### Beli (beliapp.co)
- Unique: A vs B comparative rating methodology
- Strong gamification for Gen Z (levels, badges, leaderboards)
- Weakness: No editorial authority

### Resy (resy.com)
- Strengths: Editorial Hit Lists, post-visit push notifications
- SaaS model: restaurants pay for booking insights
- Combines booking + editorial curation

### Tabelog (tabelog.com, Japan)
- 35M monthly users
- Weighted ratings: author credibility affects review weight
- Dish-level ratings (planned for Plavno future)
- Weakness: Japan only

### Paire Appétit
- Best ML taste profile model: 5-dimensional (Richness, Spice, Tradition, Adventure, Indulgence)
- Weakness: Quiz only at onboarding; Plavno runs it per session

## Market Gaps Identified

### In Russia
- No authoritative premium guide post-Michelin exit (2022)
- No transparent rating methodology
- No ML-personalization
- No gamification in editorial context
- Missing social layer in guides

### Globally
- No independent premium guide in CIS region
- Review manipulation undermines trust on all major platforms
- No dish-level granularity (only venue-level)

---

## Plavno's Positioning Formula
> Plavno = Beli (gamification) + Resy (editorial voice) + Michelin (independence/status) + Tabelog (weighted ratings)

### Unique differentiators
1. ML-quiz as central navigation — not onboarding, not sidebar (first globally)
2. Dual-rating: expert panel 40% + community weighted by visit credibility 35%
3. Fully transparent methodology — scoring formula published
4. Named expert recommendations (each curator identified)
5. Editorial authority + gamification combined

## Market Data
- Global market: $8.4B
- Russia: ~$120M (~1.4% of global)
- Premium subscriptions growing: $380M market in 2025
- 58% of users distrust purely algorithmic reviews
- AI personalization is new baseline by 2026

## Key Risks (Plavno-specific)
1. Yandex dominates distribution (80M+ MAU on Maps)
2. 56% of 18-35 year olds discover restaurants via TikTok/Instagram first
3. Lemon Guide simultaneous Moscow launch fall 2026
4. Restaurant guide category: 4-7% DAU/MAU vs 60%+ for delivery apps
5. High review manipulation risk
